CAST OF MARIA CLARA & IBARRA

The production of the historical portal fantasy drama "Maria Clara at Ibarra", based on José Rizal's novels, began with the announcement of its lead cast on July 8, 2022.

Barbie Forteza, Dennis Trillo, and Julie Anne San Jose were confirmed to star.

The supporting cast was revealed on August 3, 2022, followed by the release of the first teaser on September 13, 2022. To ensure authenticity, the cast received extensive coaching in Spanish dialogue from Roven Alejandro, who also portrays Tiburcio De Espadaña in the series. Julie Anne San Jose's audition was particularly noteworthy, earning unanimous approval from network executives and securing her role.

The addition of new characters for the *El Filibusterismo* arc was announced on January 16, 2023, with Khalil Ramos, Pauline Mendoza, Kim de Leon, and Julia Pascual joining the cast. A new teaser, incorporated into a revised opening theme music video, highlighted this expansion of the storyline.

MAIN CHARACTERS

Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra

Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra, portrayed by Dennis Trillo: A young man who returns to his hometown of San Diego after seven years of studying abroad. He is intelligent, idealistic, and passionate about improving the lives of his people. He is in love with Maria Clara.

María Clara "Klay" Infantes

María Clara "Klay" Infantes, portrayed by Barbie Forteza, is a young woman who enters the world of Juan Crisóstomo Ibarra and the other characters in the film Noli Me Tangere. She is a unique character, perhaps a modern-day interpretation of the original Maria Clara, who brings a fresh perspective to the story.

María Clara "Clarita" de los Santos y Alba

Maria Clara, portrayed by Julie Anne San Jose: A beautiful and virtuous young woman who is engaged to Ibarra. She is kind, compassionate, and deeply religious. She is torn between her love for Ibarra and her duty to her family and society.

TOP SUPPORTING CHARACTERS

Fidel de los Reyes y Maglipol, more commonly known as "Fidel de los Reyes", is the playboy friend of Crisostomo Ibarra. While he initially seems original character in Maria Clara At Ibarra that does not appear in Noli Me Tangere, he is later revealed to be a version of the side character Albino.

He is portrayed by David Licauco.

Narcisa Infantes-Asuncion, or Narsing, is the mother of Maria Clara Infantes and Elias Infantes-Asuncion.

She is portrayed by Manilyn Reynes.

Jose R. Torres, often addressed as "Mr. Torres" or "Professor Torres", is a Filipino college professor at the Pamantasan ng Lungsod ng Maynila. He is responsible for transporting Maria Clara Infantes into the world of Noli Me Tangere.

He is portrayed by Lou Veloso.

Santiago de los Santos, more commonly known in the town of San Diego as "Kapitan Tiago", is the legal father of Maria Clara.

He is portrayed by Juan Rodrigo.

Isabel Alba is the aunt and primary caretaker of Maria Clara and the cousin of Kapitan Tiago. She is the head housekeeper in the De Los Santos homestead.

She portrayed by Ces Quesada.

Elias is a fugitive from San Diego. He is deeply devoted to his homeland, the Philippines, and is driven by a strong sense of nationalism. He recognizes the injustices suffered by his fellow countrymen under Spanish colonial rule and fights for the liberation and betterment of the Filipino people.

He is portrayed by Rocco Nacino.

Narcisa, also known as "Sisa", is the mother of Crispin and Basilio in Noli Me Tangere. When one of her sons go missing, she becomes insane in search of Crispin.

She is portrayed by Andrea Torres.

Basilio is the son of Sisa and Pedro and the older brother of Crispin. He served as one of the church sacristans in San Diego, and later, as one of Simoun's allies.

He is portrayed by Stanley Abuloc as a child and by Khalil Ramos as an adult.

Padre Dámaso is a complex and controversial character in Philippine literature. He represents the oppressive power of the Spanish friars during the colonial period. His actions and words reflect the deep-seated prejudice and discrimination against Filipinos, particularly the indios.

His portrayal by Tirso Cruz III further emphasizes his arrogance and cruelty, making him a memorable and impactful character in Philippine literature.

Padre Salví is a meticulous and cunning man who uses his religious stature for political influence, benefitting both himself and the church. He is often at odds with the town's military ensign, volleying back and forth for power over San Diego and its citizens. He orchestrated the downfall of Ibarra and lusts for Maria Clara. He attempted to shoot Ibarra, but accidentally shot Maria Clara in the end.
